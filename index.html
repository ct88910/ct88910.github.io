Some Stuff I've Done

Polytech Paramedic Paradocs

A program built for Saskatchewan Polytechnic's paramedic programs to digitize documents and make them more easily accessible to both faculty and students. The program was built as a part of our second year classes, simulating a real-world programming project using test-driven development and sprints.
Contributions include initial search features, edit forms, dynamic QR code generation, basic chat feature, and consolidation of database models early on.
Link for this one is unavailable. Sorry!
Being a project that spanned nearly 8 months, there was lots of time spent making mistakes, poor decisions, and appropriately making up for those either quickly or struggling to deal with them towards the end. The entire class cemented the importance of good communication, planning, and overall good programming practices in a production application. A full stack application, once somewhat developed, has the potential to be easily maintained should it be set up appropriately. One of my biggest takeaways is that starting something like this from the ground up can be extremely difficult, especially with no notion of what a project of this scope should look like. After a fair bit of fumbling, we as a group managed to pull together what our instructors say is "a usable application, which not many students end up with in the end."

Bubble Brawl (Saskatoon Global Game Jam 2025)

A quick game built in Godot with six others for Saskatoon Global Game Jam 2025.
Contributions include sound design and sound/audio programming.
itch.io Page
As a quick 48-hour jam, there was a fair bit of assumption that things would come together quickly and nicely, but reality set in quickly when things were not necessarily going smoothly and we ended up with not as much as we wanted to have implemented. With no prior knowledge of how much effort and time even a short game takes to make, I especially underestimated how much effort needed to go into the entire project. Learning a new language in a short period of time is also no simple feat, and I found myself scrambling to understand even something that seems as simple as Godot. While I'm still proud of the work I did, I also acknowledge that I have previously done better and had I some time to properly shake a bit of rust off, would have also been able to provide better for this project as well.

Album "Database"

A spreadsheet full of information about nearly 4000 albums to view stats related to me and my friends' favorite albums. Initially built as an exercise in Microsoft Excel to familiarize myself with formulas and programming in Visual Basic Script, it quickly grew into a passion project of hundreds of hours of manually entered data and automation into what is essentially a silly database in a spreadsheet. Alongside an easily filtered list, there are statistic tables for average rank, top and lowest albums, highest and total number of albums ranked per year and decade, and number of albums per genre and country. There are formulas abound and dynamically building spreadsheets according to a numbered list of albums, provided by the user. Attached is my personal copy, so you can judge me based on my tastes.
Download/View (only works on desktop Excel due to excessive XLOOKUP and VBS)
While this is not necessarily a professional (or necessarily useful) endeavour, it provided a great deal of insight in the workings of Excel, how databases should or could be set up, processing data, and calculating statistics, as well as just being something fun to look at and examine. While not ultimately something I would necessarily continue to develop or maintain far into the future, it may be something worth turning into a separate application for extra practice with other languages and concepts.
